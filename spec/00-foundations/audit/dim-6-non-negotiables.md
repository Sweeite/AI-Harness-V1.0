# Dimension 6 — Three Non-negotiables End-to-end

## Summary

This dimension traced the spec's three non-negotiables — (1) never lose or corrupt
knowledge, (2) never do something it shouldn't, (3) never fail silently — end-to-end
across components, checking whether each invariant's guarantees are actually backed by
a written, enforceable requirement rather than asserted in narrative, OD prose, or an
FR's summary language. Agents followed specific chains: the compliance-erasure path
(memory → backup/DR → maintenance jobs), event-delivery gap-reconciliation across
connectors (Slack/Google/GHL), the ingestion drop/discard trust-window audit, the
C6 approval-hold mechanism as it threads through synchronous custom-command
invocation, the C5 per-step execution order against C6's injection-sanitization
pipeline, and the "who watches the watcher" alerting chain (alert-engine watchdog,
management-plane staleness evaluator, DLQ-liveness heartbeat, embedding write-failure
retry queue). Six raw findings were produced: three under non-negotiable #1
(knowledge loss/corruption — backup-purge erasure gap, GHL reconciliation gap,
ingestion-audit-drift gap) and three under non-negotiables #2/#3 (custom-command
approval-hold mechanism gap, C5/C6 injection-sanitization call-site gap, and the
self-hosted-watcher/no-external-monitor gap plus the embedding write-failure queue's
missing staleness escalation).
